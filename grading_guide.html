This page features a guide to determine the quality of the golf ball

There are 4 tiers:

  • Grade A
  • Grade B
  • Grade C
  • Rejects

Grade A

Best quality - very minor scrapes, very few or no player markings Clean, perfactly playable balls that may have minor scuffs or marks, but nothing that would affect trajectory or feel of the ball. Also known as "Near Mint" and "Excellent" condition. eg 75p per ball. The Best Grade A balls will be near brand new, with no marks or scratches.

Grade B

Medium quality - small, scrapes cuts and player markings, possibly cosmetically changed and some player markings. None of thease blemishes should affect play, distance or flight path. Cheap good deal, eg 40p per ball

Grade C

Large scrapes which may affect quality of play Moderate blemishes and Best value for money, ideal for practice rounds, moderate costmetic blemishes, discolourations and some large scrapes and paint worn off. eg 20p per ball

Rejects

Ball is only suitable for use in practice, low quality, Noticeable wear, scuffs and discolouration Have splits, cracks, deformed, paint off and damaged. Thease balls are not suitable for play, but for practice shots, or shots over hazards. Price is at giveaway prices, very cheap, eg 10p each
